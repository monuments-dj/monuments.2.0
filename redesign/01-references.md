# Reference Library: Cutting-Edge Web Design 2025-2026

Curated for the monuments redesign, July 2026. Even split: 8 studios, 8 individuals.
All verified live at research time. Lane key: **simple-iconic** (tinywins lane) vs
**maximal-immersive** (lusion lane). Anchors DJ already loves: tinywins.com, lusion.co.

---

## STUDIOS

### 1. Malvah Studio — https://malvah.co
Awwwards **Studio of the Year 2025** (Cape Town, ~10 people). Their thesis is literally
"confidence needs no decoration": big bold sans headlines, huge whitespace, hero is a work
carousel not a manifesto wall, services are four plain words. Motion budget spent only on
hero + case thumbnails; the frame stays quiet so the work reads loud.
**Steal:** the restraint model itself. One type family, one accent, "Play reel" inline
trigger that takes over full-bleed. Case-study-first nav.
**Lane:** simple-iconic. Best current proof that award-winning does not mean heavy.

### 2. Darkroom — https://darkroom.engineering
Ex-Studio Freight; the people who make **Lenis** (already in DJ's stack). Dark,
mono-flavored type, sparse sections: manifesto, exactly three featured projects,
testimonials, OSS, services. A public activity log makes the studio feel alive.
**Steal:** three-project homepage cap + "see all". Changelog-as-proof-of-life (trivially
static). Own-your-stack performance bragging fits DJ's hand-coded pitch.
**Lane:** simple-iconic, hard. Most directly copyable site on this list.

### 3. basement.studio — https://basement.studio
Argentina/US studio for Vercel, Linear, Cursor, MrBeast. Brutalist confidence: mono +
grotesque pairing, black/white base with acid accents, oversized kinetic headlines,
marquee strips, cursor swaps, hover-reveal cards. Loud, but mostly DOM/CSS in execution.
**Steal:** marquee rows, mono labels + giant display type, sticky section headers,
client-logo wall as credibility engine. "Loud but light."
**Lane:** middle, leaning maximal in attitude.

### 4. Buttermax — https://buttermax.net
Active Theory's production sub-brand. CSSDA Website of the Year 2024. One joke (butter)
executed flawlessly: one saturated hue, gooey elastic motion, kinetic type.
**Steal:** the one-idea discipline. Pick a single ownable visual metaphor and let one
color do the branding. Mouse-follow elasticity fakeable with spring physics on transforms.
**Lane:** iconic-maximal hybrid: lusion-lane tech wearing a tinywins-lane concept.

### 5. Unseen Studio — https://unseen.co
Bristol/London. Kinetic lettermark (U N S E E N animates constantly). Interaction verbs
surfaced as UI copy: "Drag to explore", "Click & Hold". A separate "World" section
quarantines the playground so the work index stays fast.
**Steal:** labeled interactions (micro-copy makes modest interactions feel designed).
Fast index + one indulgent page is the right architecture for a lean build. DJ's /lab
already is this; lean in.
**Lane:** middle-to-maximal with clean IA underneath.

### 6. Obys Agency — https://obys.agency
Ukrainian, typography-driven. Numbered editorial work index (01-19) with category tags,
live CET clock in the footer, grid discipline throughout. Typography IS the interface.
**Steal:** the numbered work-index pattern (semantic HTML list, huge display type,
hover swaps a preview). Live clock + (c)2026 micro-details signal a maintained site.
**Lane:** simple-iconic, typography-maximal. Best-in-class for "type is the design."

### 7. Exo Ape — https://www.exoape.com
Dutch, cinematic brand experiences (Columbia Pictures). THE reference for slow cinematic
scroll rhythm: full-bleed imagery, serif/sans editorial pairing, "Scroll to explore"
pacing, alternating image-dominant and text-dominant blocks like a title sequence.
**Steal:** parallax on images only (media moves, type stays), staggered line-by-line
headline reveals, alternating full-bleed/contained cadence, image-first case cards
(title + year + one line). NOTE: repo already has an EXOAPE-PASS.md; this stays the
single most relevant studio reference.
**Lane:** the exact midpoint. Minimal layout, immersive feel.

### 8. Immersive Garden — https://www.immersive-g.com
Paris. Awwwards **Agency of the Year 2025** (Louis Vuitton, Cartier, OMEGA).
Luxury maximalism with editorial control: minimal type over rich scenes, 20+ cases under
two narrative sections, whitespace keeps heavy visuals from feeling heavy.
**Steal:** the content architecture (philosophy line, then sequential case studies ARE
the site). Progressive reveal reads expensive and is ~30 lines of IntersectionObserver.
Study how they caption luxury work in tiny quiet type.
**Lane:** maximal-immersive, the 2025-crowned version. Reference for feel, not build.

## INDIVIDUALS

### 9. Salomon Ligthelm — https://ligthelm.work
Commercial + music video director (Ford, Nike, The Weeknd), 2026 Kodak Director of the
Year, Gold Cannes Lion, Awwwards SOTD. Radical restraint: text-first project index
organized by taxonomy (Selected / Narrative / Music Videos / Commercials), poetic
metadata per project (locations, coordinates, "Shot on Film"). Information page:
narrative bio, then reps, then a chronological awards list 2016-2026.
**Steal:** (a) the text-index work page with hover-reveal video, reads instantly senior;
(b) metadata-as-texture (year, location, format on each project); (c) the about formula:
story-first bio then plain chronological credits, maps perfectly to DJ's voice;
(d) credibility logos as a quiet footer band.
**Lane:** simple-iconic. Proof a director site wins SOTD with almost no ornament.

### 10. Gavin Schneider Productions — https://gavinschneider.com
Cape Town director/producer shingle. Awwwards SOTD Mar 2026 + FWA. Strict two-color
palette (#FBFBFB / #000000), Fashion / Film & TV / Photography tabs, one celebrated
preloader, footage and typography do everything.
**Steal:** two-color discipline (maps to ink/paper), category-tab work index, one
exquisite preloader + fast pages beats heavy WebGL for a production audience. This is
the closest analogue to Monuments itself and the competitive benchmark.
**Lane:** simple-iconic, aggressively.

### 11. Ash Thorp — https://altcinc.com
Director/designer (The Batman, Ghost in the Shell titles, Nike, McLaren) fronting his
personal shingle ALT Creative. Work-forward grid; every card carries a format label
("short film", "title sequence", "commercial"). Personal-interest nav items humanize.
**Steal:** format-classification labels on work cards (communicates range the way a
client-side producer scans), the person-as-studio-brand model, a permitted personal
obsessions section.
**Lane:** simple-iconic; imagery carries the weight.

### 12. Sergey Lisovskiy — https://lisovskiy.work
CD / designer-developer, 15+ years (Canon, Kia). Best career-arc communication in the
set: one long page, all projects listed reverse-chronologically 2026 back to 2014,
staff roles marked with an asterisk, ten stated working values, signs off "Peace."
**Steal:** the reverse-chronological everything-list as a de facto career timeline
(DJ's UX > agency > CD > studio arc as one scannable column, pure HTML/CSS); the
asterisk convention for role types; "Fields" + "Values" sections.
**Lane:** simple-iconic; content architecture is the star.

### 13. Dennis Snellenberg — https://dennissnellenberg.com
The most-cloned personal portfolio of the decade, still the benchmark. Rotating
multilingual hello, one confident value prop, four featured cards (name/service/year)
with hover video, the famous magnetic contact pill, live local-time footer.
**Steal:** magnetic/sticky contact button (vanilla lerp toward cursor); live clock +
location footer ("real person, available" signal); minimal card schema that scales;
rounded-slab section transitions.
**Lane:** simple-iconic with lush micro-interactions. Closest existing model to the
tinywins/lusion midpoint DJ wants.

### 14. Niccolò Miranda — https://www.niccolomiranda.com
Digital art director (Prada, On), 9x SOTD. Oversized kinetic type, drag carousel, each
project gets a custom logotype in the index, an awards/stats block, peer testimonials
on the homepage.
**Steal:** the stats counter block (DJ's 4x / 160M+ / Emmy / 50 contractors belongs in
exactly this treatment); a testimonial strip from agency collaborators; per-case
typographic lockups so the index feels like a title-card reel.
**Lane:** leans maximal. Borrow components, not the density.

### 15. Adam Ho — https://www.adamho.com
Independent designer (ex Cash App). One numbered-grid page whose modules reshuffle on
every reload; looping GIFs instead of heavy video; dry humor mid-bio; refined type;
site is versioned ("v10, 2.0").
**Steal:** numbered-index single-page architecture; shuffle-on-load (a shuffled array +
CSS grid); GIF/short-loop thumbnails instead of video embeds; voice-in-the-margins copy
matching DJ's anti-jargon tone; version your site like a product.
**Lane:** simple-iconic, typographic; personality via system, not spectacle.

### 16. Rauno Freiberg — https://rauno.me
Interaction designer at Vercel; the industry reference for micro-interaction craft.
The "Craft" page (a feed of small playable interaction experiments) is the pattern
everyone imitates. Bio replaced by a stacked manifesto ("Make it fast. Make it
beautiful. Make it soulful. Make it.").
**Steal:** the craft/lab framing for /lab (experiments read as senior craft, not
doodles); manifesto-as-bio stack for the about hero; the standard that every
interaction ships at 60fps or not at all.
**Lane:** extreme simple-iconic; the motion-quality bar.

## Cross-cutting patterns (encode these)
1. **Lenis smooth scroll is the shared substrate** of nearly everything above. Already in DJ's stack.
2. **Three featured projects max on home** + a full work index page. Keeps first paint light.
3. **Interaction micro-copy** ("scroll to explore", "click + hold") is the cheapest perceived-craft multiplier.
4. **Numbered/indexed work lists with hover previews** are the dominant 2025-26 typographic pattern.
5. **One ownable color beats a palette.** (Monuments already has this: denim, with rose as punctuation.)
6. **Proof-of-life details**: live clock, changelog, (c)2026, versioned site. Signals an active studio.
7. **Seniority is communicated structurally, not adjectivally**: chronological lists, awards tallies, logo bands, stats blocks. Nobody writes "seasoned leader."
8. **The winning motion budget in 2025-26 is small and precise**: magnetic buttons, hover video reveals, kinetic hero type, one great preloader. Full WebGL is a creative-technologist signal, not a CD signal.
9. **Director-lane sites are the most restrained of all** (Ligthelm, Schneider): black/white, type-led, footage talks. Argues for restraint on /work and spending the flourish on hero, transitions, /lab.
